## Design Context

### Users

DevOps/SRE engineers, cloud/infrastructure professionals, and tech industry practitioners. They arrive from Hangops Slack, Twitter, or search — often mid-incident or post-mortem. They're looking for shared experience, catharsis, and occasionally real reference material about past disasters. They know what a tire fire feels like because they've lived through them.

### Brand Personality

The personality shifts fluidly between three modes depending on the page and moment:

- **Sardonic, communal, honest** — dry wit, shared pain, no sugarcoating
- **Chaotic, playful, loud** — unhinged energy, meme-adjacent, attention-grabbing
- **Punk, defiant, raw** — anti-corporate, rough edges on purpose, zine aesthetic

Sometimes one dominates. Sometimes all three at once. The site should feel like gallows humor among colleagues — an irreverent encyclopedia you genuinely enjoy reading, where humor is therapeutic and communal (#hugops energy).

### Aesthetic Direction

The site has **two distinct visual personalities**:

**Original pages — Refined Minimal:**
- Clean, fast, readable. Better typography and spacing than default Jekyll, but never precious.
- The content speaks; the design stays out of the way.

**WIP pages — Retro Industrial:**
- Old-school terminal meets brutalist web design meets cyberpunk dashboard.
- Dark backgrounds, monospace type, raw HTML energy, harsh contrasts.
- Neon/amber accents on dark. Data-dense. Function over form with personality.
- Think htop crossed with brutalistwebsites.com — not polished, not ironic-retro, just *raw*.

**Anti-references (what this must NEVER look like):**
- Corporate SaaS (no gradient heroes, no stock photos, no "Schedule a Demo" energy)
- Social media / Web3 (no cards-and-likes feeds, no crypto aesthetic)
- Overly polished startup (no Stripe-level micro-animations, no Apple minimalism — too clean kills the vibe)

**Colors:**
- No hard constraints. Current blue accent (#2a7ae2) works for original pages.
- WIP pages should use whatever serves the dark/industrial direction — amber, orange, terminal green are all fair game.
- Fire palette is thematically appropriate but not mandatory.

### Design Principles

1. **Personality over polish** — Rough edges are features, not bugs. The site should feel like it was made by people who've been in the trenches, not a design agency.

2. **Content is the fire** — Every design choice serves readability and discovery. If a visual element doesn't help someone find or absorb a tire fire story, cut it.

3. **Two modes, one voice** — Original (minimal) and WIP (industrial) pages look different but feel like the same community. The humor and honesty carry across both.

4. **Accessible by default** — WCAG AAA preferred. Good contrast, reduced motion support, semantic HTML, keyboard navigation. Accessibility isn't optional even for a joke site.

5. **Fast and light** — Static site, static priorities. No heavy frameworks, no unnecessary JS, no layout shift. Pages should load like they're served from localhost.

### Accessibility

- Target: WCAG AAA compliance
- Reduced motion: respect `prefers-reduced-motion`
- Color contrast: meet AAA ratios (7:1 for normal text, 4.5:1 for large text)
- Semantic HTML throughout
- Keyboard navigable
- Alt text on all images
- No information conveyed by color alone
