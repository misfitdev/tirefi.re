<div class="content-section">
    <h2>The Ritual</h2>
    <p>System Administrator Appreciation Day is the last Friday of July. It was established in 2000. The observation, for most of its existence, has gone like this: the ops team forwards the Wikipedia article to each other, someone posts the original 2000-era SysAdmin Day website in the team chat, someone else says "happy sysadmin day" and means it, and then a VP sends a Slack message later that afternoon after their assistant reminded them it was a thing. The VP calls the team "the IT guys." The ops team is not the IT team. This is fine. This is every year.</p>

    <p>In a good year, someone from engineering says thanks and actually means it. This is the ceiling. This is as good as it gets. It is worth more than the gift card to a coffee chain that gets sent in normal years.</p>
</div>

<div class="content-section">
    <h2>I Am Jack's Cost Center</h2>
    <p>Fight Club: "I am Jack's complete lack of surprise." Operations: I am Jack's cost center. The accounting designation means money flows in and nothing billable flows out. You are an expense on a spreadsheet. This is technically accurate and completely wrong as a description of what you do, but the spreadsheet doesn't have a column for "prevented the production database from being exposed to the internet for three years running."</p>

    <p>The cost center framing means every budget cycle starts from "justify your existence" instead of "what do you need." It means the team is sized for normal operations and not for the incident that will happen. It means the monitoring tooling request has been in the queue since Q1 because it's hard to explain the return on investment of something that prevents a thing that hasn't happened yet.</p>

    <p>The journey from sysadmin to DevOps engineer to SRE to Platform Engineer is largely a story of rebranding without commensurate budget increases. Each title change came with a new set of responsibilities, a new vocabulary, and roughly the same number of people trying to cover roughly the same amount of surface area. The cost center problem did not resolve with the title. It just got new terminology.</p>
</div>

<div class="content-section">
    <h2>Pete Cheslock Was Right Day</h2>
    <p>Pete Cheslock Was Right Day is observed whenever someone on the ops team says "I told you this would happen" and is correct — which is more often than the quarterly retrospective will acknowledge, and more often than leadership's memory of the conversation will confirm. It is not on the last Friday of July. It is observed irregularly, usually during or immediately after an incident, and the observation is typically silent.</p>

    <p>Happy SysAdmin Day. The uptime you preserved today is invisible. That's the job.</p>
</div>
